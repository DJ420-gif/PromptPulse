What Grammarly AI Does in 2026

Grammarly in 2026 is significantly more than the grammar checker it launched as. Beyond spelling and grammar it now provides real-time tone adjustment that rewrites sentences to match specified tones — professional, casual, confident, diplomatic — across Gmail, Google Docs, LinkedIn, Slack and over 500,000 apps and websites via the browser extension. The full AI rewrite feature can restructure entire paragraphs for better flow and clarity not just fix individual errors. It generates email replies, meeting summaries and document drafts from prompts within the extension. The plagiarism checker compares against 16 billion web pages. In a study involving native English-speaking specialists Grammarly scored an average of 83 out of 100 on writing quality improvement — only 0.4% of punctuation suggestions and 2.9% of spelling suggestions required correction in independent testing.

02

The Core Question — Does Claude Make Grammarly Unnecessary

This is the question most 2026 reviewers are asking and the honest answer is that it depends entirely on your workflow. Claude and ChatGPT produce better generative writing output than Grammarly when you are drafting from scratch. But Grammarly's value is not primarily in generation — it is in inline editing of writing you have already produced, working everywhere you type rather than requiring you to paste content into a chat window. The browser extension that corrects your email as you type in Gmail, fixes your Slack message as you write it, and improves your LinkedIn post in real time is a fundamentally different workflow from copying text to Claude and pasting back. For professionals who write many short-form communications daily — emails, messages, posts — Grammarly's contextual inline assistance saves more time than Claude for those specific tasks.

03

Pricing — Free vs Premium vs Business

Grammarly Free provides real-time grammar and spelling corrections — still the most capable free writing correction tool available. Premium at $12 per month adds full-sentence rewrites, tone detection, advanced style suggestions and plagiarism detection. Business plans at $15 per person per month add team analytics, shared style guides and priority support. The comparison to Claude Pro at $20 per month is relevant: for writers who primarily need grammar correction and tone improvement Grammarly at $12 is better value. For writers who need full document generation and research Claude is better value. Most serious writers use both — Grammarly for editing and Claude for drafting — at $32 per month total.

04

Grammarly vs QuillBot vs ProWritingAid

Grammarly leads on integration breadth — 500,000+ apps versus QuillBot's more limited coverage. QuillBot is better for academic paraphrasing and is the preferred tool for students rewriting source material. ProWritingAid offers more detailed writing statistics and a lifetime plan option that eliminates recurring subscription costs — attractive for writers who want to pay once. For professionals who write across Gmail, Slack, LinkedIn and Google Docs daily Grammarly's universal coverage justifies the monthly cost over alternatives with narrower integration. The recommended stack: Grammarly for editing and inline correction everywhere, Claude or ChatGPT for generation and research.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Grammarly worth it in 2026?
Yes for professionals who write across many platforms daily. The browser extension that corrects writing inline everywhere you type is a different value proposition from Claude which requires copying and pasting. Most serious writers use both — $32/month combined.
Does Claude replace Grammarly?
For drafting from scratch yes. For inline editing of writing you have already produced across Gmail, Slack and LinkedIn simultaneously no. Grammarly and Claude complement each other — Grammarly for editing, Claude for generating.
How much does Grammarly cost in 2026?
Free for basic grammar and spelling. Premium $12/month for full rewrites, tone detection and plagiarism checking. Business $15/person/month for team features. Annual billing provides ~40% discount on Premium.
Grammarly vs QuillBot — which is better?
Grammarly for professionals needing corrections across all daily writing apps. QuillBot for students and academics primarily needing paraphrasing and citation support. Grammarly has better integration breadth. QuillBot is better for academic rewriting tasks.
Is Grammarly free plan good enough?
For basic grammar and spelling yes — it catches most common errors. For sentence restructuring, tone adjustment, advanced style suggestions and plagiarism detection you need Premium. The free plan is the best free grammar tool available.
