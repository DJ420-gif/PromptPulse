01

What Changed in Gemini 3.1 Pro vs Previous Gemini Versions

Gemini 3.1 Pro represents Google's most significant model improvement since Gemini 1.5 Pro. The intelligence index score from Artificial Analysis places Gemini 3.1 Pro Preview at the top tier alongside GPT-5.4 — ahead of Claude Sonnet 4.6 and Grok 4.20 on this composite measure. Claude Sonnet 4.6 gave Anthropic a model that performs at near-Opus level but at Sonnet pricing — and Gemini 3.1 Pro is Google's answer to that pressure. Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite is highlighted for speed and cost-efficiency focusing on latency and cost per capability rather than raw performance making it the most accessible entry point into the Gemini 3.1 family.

02

Benchmark Scores and Intelligence Index

Gemini 3.1 Pro Preview leads among 136 reasoning models with an Intelligence Index score of 57 from Artificial Analysis. GPT-5.4 at xhigh and Claude Opus 4.6 at max are listed as the highest intelligence models in the same comparison. On output speed Gemini 2.5 Flash-Lite maintains the fastest latency at 0.75 seconds to first token — significantly ahead of competitors. The key advantage Google maintains is the multi-modal training depth — Gemini models are trained natively on text code images and audio in ways that give consistent advantages on mixed-media tasks. Google Search integration for real-time grounding gives Gemini 3.1 Pro a factual accuracy advantage on current events over Claude and GPT which have training cutoffs.

03

Pricing and Free Tier Comparison

Gemini 3.1 Pro is available via Google AI Studio and Vertex AI. The free tier remains the most generous of any major AI model family — Gemini 1.5 Pro with 1M token context is available free with daily usage limits that exceed what most individual users need. Gemini Advanced at $20/month through Google One adds Gemini 3.1 Pro access and deeper Google Workspace integration. For developers the API pricing is competitive particularly for high-volume use where Gemini Flash models at $0.25 per million tokens provide cost efficiency no Western closed model matches. The 3.1 Flash-Lite variant focuses on minimising latency and cost — positioned directly against GPT-5.4 mini and Claude Haiku pricing tiers.

04

Who Should Use Gemini 3.1 Pro

Gemini 3.1 Pro is most valuable for developers already in the Google ecosystem who need deep Google Search grounding for factual accuracy on current events. For multi-modal workflows combining text images and audio Gemini's native multi-modal architecture gives consistent advantages over models that bolt vision capabilities onto text-only bases. For the highest quality complex TypeScript and production coding Claude Opus 4.6 and GPT-5.4 still lead. For the best balance of intelligence score and Google ecosystem integration Gemini 3.1 Pro is the strongest Google AI has shipped and worth evaluating if your previous Gemini experience was disappointing.

05

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Gemini 3.1 Pro better than Claude?
On the Artificial Analysis intelligence index Gemini 3.1 Pro Preview leads alongside GPT-5.4. For complex TypeScript coding Claude Opus 4.6 still leads. For Google Search-grounded factual accuracy and multi-modal tasks Gemini 3.1 Pro has advantages.
How much does Gemini 3.1 Pro cost?
Gemini Advanced with 3.1 Pro access costs $20/month via Google One. API pricing via Vertex AI is competitive. Gemini Flash variants start at $0.25 per million tokens. Free tier with Gemini 1.5 Pro remains available.
What is the difference between Gemini 3.1 Pro and Flash?
Gemini 3.1 Pro is the highest capability model — highest intelligence index score. Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite is optimised for speed and cost-efficiency. Flash-Lite has the lowest latency at 0.75 seconds to first token of any major model.
Does Gemini 3.1 have real-time search?
Yes — Google Search integration provides real-time grounding for factual accuracy on current events. This gives Gemini 3.1 a significant advantage over Claude and GPT on questions about recent events where training cutoffs limit competitor accuracy.
Gemini 3.1 vs GPT-5.4 — which is better?
Both sit at the top of the Artificial Analysis intelligence index. GPT-5.4 has a 1M context window advantage. Gemini 3.1 has real-time Google Search grounding. For coding tasks the gap is smaller than previous generations — both competitive at the frontier.
