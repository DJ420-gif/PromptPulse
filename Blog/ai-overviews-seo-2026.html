01

The Scale of What Has Changed

Google AI Overviews reach 2 billion monthly users across 200 countries in 40 languages. ChatGPT processes over 1 billion queries per day and drives 77% of all AI-driven website referral traffic. Perplexity accounts for approximately 15% of AI referral traffic. Together these AI search tools represent a fundamental change in how content is discovered. For developer-focused content specifically — the audience most likely to use AI search — the shift is even more pronounced. Getting cited by these tools is no longer a nice-to-have. It is essential visibility.

02

The Content Signals That Trigger AI Citations

Research across thousands of pages cited by AI Overviews reveals consistent patterns. Pages that answer a specific question in the first two sentences get cited significantly more often than pages that bury the answer. Content with specific statistics and sourced data gets cited more than vague generalisations. Pages updated within the past two months receive 28% more citations than older content. And clear heading structure that maps to specific questions — not clever headings but literal question-answer headings — performs best in both traditional and AI search.

03

The Technical Changes That Make the Biggest Difference

FAQ schema markup is the highest-impact technical change you can make. When Google sees FAQ schema, it understands your content as a set of question-answer pairs — exactly what AI Overviews pull from. Every page should have a FAQ section with schema markup above the footer. Author markup matters — pages that demonstrate real human expertise with named authors and verifiable credentials get cited more often. And canonical URLs must be correct — duplicate content issues cause AI tools to ignore pages even when the content is excellent.

04

The Content Strategy That Gets You Cited

Build topical authority not individual pages. AI tools cite sites that demonstrate comprehensive coverage of a topic — not sites with one great article. For PromptPulse this means owning the full AI tool review cluster: individual reviews, comparisons, how-to guides and news updates all interlinked and pointing to a central pillar page. Every page you create should link to and from related pages. The depth of your topic coverage is more important than the quality of any single article.

05

Frequently Asked Questions

What are Google AI Overviews?
AI Overviews are AI-generated summaries that appear at the top of Google search results, synthesising information from multiple sources. They now appear on 13% of all searches and reach 2 billion monthly users globally.
How do I get my website cited in Google AI Overviews?
Add FAQ schema markup, answer questions directly in your first paragraph, include specific statistics and data, update content frequently, and build topical authority by covering your subject comprehensively across multiple linked pages.
Does SEO still matter in 2026 with AI search?
Yes — but it has evolved. Traditional ranking factors still apply but AI citation optimisation is now equally important. The sites getting cited by AI Overviews and ChatGPT are the same sites that rank well in traditional search — quality, authority and structure.
How often should I update content for AI Overviews?
Monthly updates are optimal. Pages updated within the past two months receive 28% more AI citations than older content. Adding new data, updated scores or fresh examples counts as meaningful updates.
Does FAQ schema help with Google AI Overviews?
Yes significantly. FAQ schema tells Google your content is structured as question-answer pairs — exactly what AI Overviews pull from. Every page should have a FAQ section with proper schema markup.

  • FAQ schema markup is the highest-impact single technical change

  • Topical authority beats single great articles — cover your subject comprehensively
